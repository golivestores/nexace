Where It All Began

Nexace started in a small woodworking studio outside Melbourne. Our founder — a former dancer and studio owner — had spent years teaching reformer pilates on equipment built for clinics: loud, imposing, and far too heavy to live in a home. She wanted to change that.

She wanted a reformer quiet enough for an apartment, warm enough for a living room, and exacting enough to stand in for the studio she had trained in for two decades.

The brief, from first sketch to final prototype, was strict. Every spring had to register the same four resistance tiers she had learned on. Every rail had to glide in silence. Every frame — laminated from quarter-sawn maple and brushed aircraft-grade aluminum — had to earn its place in a room, not dominate it.

The first NX901 took nine months and thirty-four revisions.

The turning point came in 2021, when the studios she had trained in shut their doors for the last time. Our earliest customers — instructors, new mothers, physiotherapists — began writing to say the reformer had carried them through the hardest months of their lives.

We realized what we were really making wasn't equipment. It was a doorway — a way back to one's own body, on one's own hours.

Today, each Nexace reformer is still finished by hand in our original workshop. The Classic, the MI, and the Tower are each tested against a studio-grade checklist — spring calibration, rail glide, headrest pitch, strap tension — before any one of them leaves our doors.

We ship to thirty-five countries. We answer every email ourselves. And we're still making them the way we started — slowly, carefully, one at a time.

"We believe movement is a daily practice — something that should fit inside your home as naturally as morning light through a window. Every Nexace reformer is built to make that practice possible, decade after decade."

Move with precision. Train with confidence. Live with intention.
